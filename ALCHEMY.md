# Alchemy Design

Authoritative reference for the principle system, field propagation, transformation,
and combination rules. Substance profiles and combination outcomes TBD.

---

## Principle System

Every entity optionally carries a `PrincipleProfile` — 10 signed 8-bit integer values,
one per principle. Stored as `ComponentStore<PrincipleProfile>`; not all entities need
one. The values express the entity's alignment with each principle — magnitude indicates
how strongly defined the entity is along that axis. Opposite principles can coexist on
the same entity.

Mana is the Life principle.

| Pair | Principle A | Principle B |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Caloric** | Heat | Cold |
| **Aqueous** | Wet | Dry |
| **Vital** | Life | Death |
| **Galvanic** | Positive | Negative |
| **Cohesion** | Adhesive | Repellent |

## Field System

Entities with a `PrincipleProfile` radiate fields into surrounding space each tick.
The field at any location is the sum of contributions from nearby entities, with
falloff by distance. Fields propagate in all three spatial dimensions (x, y, z).

**Tiles carry no field information.** Fields are computed each tick into a transient
grid (per tile resolution, 1 tile = 1 metre) and discarded after application. Nothing
is stored on tiles.

Fields are independent — each principle field only affects its corresponding principle
value in nearby entities. There are no cross-effects between fields.

### Propagation per field

| Field | Propagation model | Directional bias |
|-------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Heat** | Radiates outward | Stronger upward (convection) |
| **Cold** | Radiates outward | Stronger downward |
| **Wet** | Diffusion through entities | Outward and downward; slowly dissipates without active source |
| **Dry** | Diffusion through entities | Outward and upward |
| **Galvanic (Positive/Negative)** | Real-world electrodynamics — inverse square falloff; current flows through conductive paths; magnetic fields generated by moving charge | Isotropic |
| **Cohesion (Adhesive/Repellent)** | Physical forces acting on entities — pulls or pushes entities toward/away from source | Isotropic |

Falloff functions are modelled on real-world physics at 1 tile = 1 metre scale.
Specific falloff curves to be determined per field during implementation.

## Transformation

A substance's principle profile shifts over time as it absorbs the local field.
The rate of transformation depends on the substance and the field intensity — stronger
fields drive faster transformation; some substances are inherently more reactive than
others.

**Attractor model** — each named substance has a canonical `PrincipleProfile`. After
any profile change, the entity's profile is compared against all known attractors.
The entity crystallises into whichever substance has the closest profile in 10D
principle space. There is no threshold — it always becomes the nearest attractor.

**Example — fire spreading through grass:**
1. Fire entity radiates Heat field
2. Grass entity absorbs Heat; its Heat value rises
3. Profile is now closest to Grass-on-fire attractor → transforms
4. Grass-on-fire loses Life over time
5. Profile is now closest to Ash attractor → transforms

## Combination

When two substances occupy the same tile — either one entering a tile already
occupied by another, or two entering the same tile in the same tick — they combine.

The result profile has three inputs:

1. **Weighted average** of the two substance profiles, weighted by their respective
   magnitudes (sum of absolute values across all 10 principles). A more strongly
   defined substance pulls the result further toward itself.
2. **Local field** at the tile where combination occurs — the ambient principle field
   modifies the result.
3. **Catalytic field** — the summed principle contributions of nearby entities that
   are not consumed in the process. Catalysts influence the result without being depleted.

The result profile is then compared against all attractors and crystallises into the
nearest named substance. Rate of reaction depends on the substances involved — some
combinations are instantaneous, others gradual.

**Consumed vs catalyst:**
- Substances being transformed and fuels are consumed
- Catalyst entities (tiles, structures, placed entities) contribute to the catalytic
  field without being depleted

## Equipment

Players shape field environments by arranging entities and structures in space.
There are no explicit vessel entities or recipe patterns — the field intensity at
a location determines what transformation occurs. Better-built structures produce
stronger, purer fields and faster transformations.
